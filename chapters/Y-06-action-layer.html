The Action Layer

Not team averages. Not segment rollups. Each individual's contribution, capacity, development gaps, and trajectory — live, granular, and actionable in the moment it matters.
The Knowledge Vault records what happened. The action layer makes something happen next. Every FLM gets a live, per-person read: contribution, capacity, development gaps, trajectory. It doesn't report after the fact — it intervenes while the outcome is still changeable. Coaching intelligence, in-workflow nudges, and inspection tools reach the manager in the moment they can still act — not in a quarterly post-mortem.
Layer 1 · Know
Awareness & Understanding
Content, campaigns, comms. Why this matters, what's changing, what's expected.
Layer 2 · Learn
Competency & Skill
Role-based curriculum, certifications, practice sandboxes, graduation gates.
Layer 3 · Do
In-Workflow Action
Agentic assists, coaching prompts, deal intelligence, inspection tools, feedback loops.
Per-Individual Condition — Four Lenses
◎
Individual Condition Report
One person. Four lenses. Updated live — not monthly, not quarterly.
▲
Contribution
What this person is producing — right now. Pipeline generated, deals closed, expansion sourced, activities completed. Measured against their targets, not the team average.
→Pipeline created vs. quota ratio
→Win rate & deal velocity vs. cohort
→Activity-to-outcome conversion
◆
Capacity
Bandwidth, not busyness. Current book of business, deal load, territory coverage — is this person overloaded, underutilized, or right-sized? The FLM knows before the rep says anything.
→Active deal count vs. ideal range
→Account coverage & whitespace
→Time-in-stage distribution
◉
Development
Where the growth edge is. Skill gaps surfaced from call patterns, certification status, playbook adoption, and coaching brief insights — specific, not speculative.
→Discovery depth score (Gong)
→MEDDPIC field completion rate
→Playbook adoption vs. delivery
↗
Trajectory
The trend line that matters most. Improving, plateauing, or declining — across every condition dimension. Period-over-period, no ambiguity.
→30/60/90-day metric trend
→Skill progression velocity
→Ramp attainment vs. timeline
